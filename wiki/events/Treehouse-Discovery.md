# Treehouse Discovery

## Overview
Calista, Cassia, and Joren discover and claim an old treehouse in Maia's garden, which becomes their secret fortress and the center of their childhood adventures.

---

## Setting

**Location:** Maia's garden, within an old oak tree  
**Time Period:** Calista's early childhood  
**Conditions:** A sunny day perfect for exploration

---

## Description

One of the children's favorite places to explore was an old treehouse nestled in the far corner of Maia's garden. The treehouse was built high among the sturdy branches of an ancient oak, its thick canopy providing a natural roof that shaded them from the artificial sunlight streaks above.

The structure itself was a patchwork of different woods, each piece telling a story of repairs and additions made over the years. Its wooden planks, worn smooth by years of use, creaked under their weight as they climbed up to their hidden sanctuary.

### The Treehouse Structure

**Upper Level (Main Floor):**
- Accessed via an old ladder
- Walls adorned with children's drawings and maps of imagined worlds
- Corners filled with cushions and blankets for comfort
- Small chests and boxes holding treasures: smooth stones, colorful feathers, trinkets
- A small wooden table for planning sessions and games
- Simple cutout windows with fabric scrap curtains

**Lower Level (The Hollow):**
- A secret area beneath the main structure
- Second entrance adding mystery and excitement
- Additional treasures and supplies
- Surrounded by natural and bioluminescent plants

## Importance

### Narrative Significance
- Creates a central location for childhood adventures
- Establishes the friend group's special bond
- Provides a sacred space that takes on new meaning after Joren's death
- Connects to Maia's garden, linking family and friendship spaces

### Thematic Significance
- The power of imagination and play
- Friendship sanctuaries that transcend ordinary life
- Building and maintaining shared spaces
- The treehouse as a symbol of childhood innocence

---

## Outcome

The treehouse becomes:
- The center of countless adventures and games
- A planning headquarters for explorations
- A place for sharing stories and secrets
- A refuge during quiet moments and rainy days
- A memorial space after Joren's death
- A sacred place Calista and Cassia return to throughout their lives

> 
> **Cassia:** "Do you think we'll ever outgrow this place?"
> 
> **Joren:** "Never. This is our sanctuary. It'll always be special. And we'll always meet here, no matter how old we get."

## Vow and Promise

The children made an unspoken promise that the treehouse would always be their meeting place, regardless of age. This promise takes on deeper meaning after Joren's death, when Calista and Cassia continue to visit and eventually adorn the walls with drawings and messages honoring his memory.
